Edgaze is in **beta**. Updates are grouped by month (**newest first**). Each month explains **what changed for you** in plain language—we do not publish internal engineering notes here. For binding terms, see Terms of Service, Privacy Policy, and the other policy pages in Docs.

The **February 2026** section is both our **initial platform / public-beta charter** (what the product is, what you should expect, and what is limited) and the first wave of product improvements after that baseline. **March** and **April** build on that foundation.

---

## April 2026

### Workflow runs, streaming, and mobile

We focused on how it **feels** to execute a workflow end to end—especially on a phone and when you are viewing a run someone else shared. Runs should reach useful output sooner, stream more smoothly, and hit fewer confusing states in the runtime UI. We also tightened edge cases in the execution flow so sessions behave more predictably when you step through longer graphs.

### Link previews, search, and brand

When you paste an Edgaze link elsewhere, previews should look clearer and more accurate. We also improved how the site presents itself in **search and social surfaces** (titles, descriptions, and imagery), and refreshed **icons and bookmark** artwork so the product looks consistent when people save or share it.

### Verified creators and account support

We introduced **verified creators** so buyers can more easily see which storefronts have gone through our verification flow. We also improved how **new and existing accounts** are set up and serviced, including support paths around **ownership changes** when the right person needs control of a creator account.

### Builder and AI providers

The workflow builder gained more options for working with **widely used AI providers** inside your graphs, so you can wire familiar models into a single published workflow. We continued fixing issues when connecting blocks, saving, and running graphs so the builder stays aligned with what buyers experience at runtime.

### License, security, and stability

We published an updated **platform license** for the Edgaze software and materials covered in Docs. If your use of the product depends on a specific license or terms snapshot, read the current version in Docs rather than relying on memory. Separately, we applied **security and reliability** improvements from our regular review cycle so the service stays safer and more dependable as usage grows.

## March 2026

### Selling, onboarding, and payouts

Creators who sell paid listings can move through **seller onboarding** and connect **payouts** through our payments partner, so earned amounts can reach your account according to the rules shown in checkout and in Docs. This is a major step beyond “everything is free in early beta”: availability, fees, and regions still depend on **current Terms** and what we enable for your account—always check the live product, not older screenshots.

### Storefront, product pages discovery

We refreshed **landing pages**, **product detail**, and **storefront** layouts so it is easier to understand what you are buying, who published it, and how pricing is shown. **Previews** for listings (including images and snippets) were improved so what you see before you click matches the page you land on.

### Blog, programs, and trust content

We added **blog** and **program-style** pages to explain what is new, highlight creators, and share context beyond individual listings. The **About** area and related pages were rewritten and reorganized so newcomers understand what Edgaze is and how we position the marketplace during beta.

### Builder backwards compatibility and runs

We kept hardening **published workflows** so older graphs still run sensibly for buyers when we ship builder changes, and we fixed issues where runtime behavior could drift from what builders expected. The goal is one consistent story: what you publish is what people run.

## February 2026 — Initial platform, public beta, and first improvements

February is when Edgaze moved from preparation to a **public beta** you could actually use: marketplace, builder, accounts, and Docs, with the commitments and limitations described below. The bullets at the end of this section are **product upgrades** we shipped in the same period on top of that foundation.

### What Edgaze is (core platform)

Edgaze is a **neutral content and discovery layer** for AI **prompts**, **workflows**, and related tools. We provide publishing, discovery, and purchase flows; we are **not** the author of third-party listings and we do not replace your own judgment about whether a workflow is right for your use case.

### Marketplace during beta

The marketplace lets you **browse**, **open product pages**, and (as the product evolved) **run or buy** workflows and prompts according to what is enabled for your account and region. In the **earliest** beta phase, most content was **free to access**; **paid listings and payouts** arrived in the **March 2026** wave above—refer to this page in order, and to the **current** Terms, for what is live **today**.

Listings are **user-generated**. Unless we show a specific badge (for example **verified creator** later on), Edge Platforms does **not** endorse individual items. Basic listing metadata (title, description, creator) helps you compare items; it is not a guarantee of quality or safety.

### Documentation and policies

Docs centralize **Terms of Service**, **Privacy Policy**, **this changelog**, and other policies so you are not hunting through blog posts for binding language. The **product UI** and **Docs** are intentionally separate: one is for doing work, the other is for understanding rules and risks.

### Creator experience and expectations

Creators can **publish**, **edit**, **unpublish**, or **remove** their own content during beta. We do **not** promise a minimum audience, ranking, or revenue. Creator tools may change or break briefly as we iterate; beta means **fast change**.

### Platform architecture (plain-English)

We run Edgaze on **production-oriented** infrastructure: sign-in, routing, delivery of pages and APIs, and internal **logging** so we can fix outages and abuse. During beta we still **do not** guarantee specific uptime, retention windows, or backward compatibility—plan accordingly for anything mission-critical.

### AI-generated content and your responsibility

Listings and runs may involve **AI-generated or AI-assisted** output. Edge Platforms does **not** verify truthfulness or safety of every run. **You** decide whether an output is fit for production, compliance, or publication.

### Known limitations and support

Beta software can have **bugs**, **missing features**, or **rough UI**. Documentation may briefly lag the live product. **Support** is limited relative to a mature paid enterprise product. If something looks wrong, checking Docs and this changelog first usually helps; policy questions belong with Terms and Privacy.

### What we are building toward

We expect to keep **improving creator workflows**, **discovery**, and **monetization tooling** (where we offer it), alongside ongoing **security** and **stability** work. Those themes show up in later months above as we ship them.

### February product improvements (same month)

On top of the charter above, we shipped concrete upgrades during February:

- **Workflow builder**: clearer **graph inspection**, smoother **inputs**, and a stronger set of **blocks** so you can express more without fighting the editor.
- **Welcome and account flows**: a **welcome** email path for new accounts and more reliable **password reset** and **profile** updates so sign-in and recovery feel less fragile.
- **Demo access**: more places to **try runs** before you fully commit to an account, where we enable it, so you can learn the product with less friction.
- **Discoverability from outside Edgaze**: refinements to how the site **reads in search** and similar surfaces, so people who have never logged in still get an accurate first impression.
