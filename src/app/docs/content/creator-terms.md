# Creator Terms

These Creator Terms apply to people and teams who publish products for distribution or sale on Edgaze.

They work alongside the general Terms of Service and the payment-related documentation in the Edgaze docs.

## On This Page

- Who qualifies as a creator
- What creators are allowed to do
- Monetization and payout expectations
- Product and conduct responsibilities
- Enforcement and account consequences

## Who These Terms Apply To

These terms apply to creators who:

- publish workflows
- publish prompt products
- enable monetization
- use Edgaze as a selling surface

If you publish products commercially on Edgaze, these terms apply to you.

### Commercial Use

These terms are specifically about creator-side publishing and monetization conduct.

## What Creators Are Allowed To Do

Creators may use Edgaze to:

- build and publish products
- set pricing within platform limits
- offer products to customers through Edgaze
- participate in the platform payout system when eligible

These permissions are conditional on following platform policy, applicable law, and payout onboarding requirements where relevant.

### Platform Permission

Edgaze gives creators a structured way to publish and sell, but those rights remain conditional.

## Creator Responsibilities

Creators are responsible for what they publish.

### Product Accuracy

Your listing, title, and description should not materially mislead customers about what the product does.

### Rights And Permissions

You must have the rights needed to publish the content, prompts, assets, branding, and product materials you use.

### Safe And Lawful Use

Your products must comply with Edgaze policy and applicable law.

### Customer Trust

Creators are expected to present products honestly and operate in a way that protects marketplace trust.

## Monetization And Earnings

The creator marketplace model is documented across the monetization pages.

### Revenue Share

Edgaze uses an 80/20 creator-platform split unless a different written commercial agreement applies.

### Payout Eligibility

Payout release depends on the supported payout onboarding and verification path.

### Sell Now, Onboard Later

Edgaze may allow creators to begin selling before payout onboarding is fully completed, but payout release still depends on eligibility.

### Operational Reality

Selling first does not remove the need to complete the required payout and verification path later.

## Fees And Pricing

Creators are responsible for choosing pricing that makes sense for their product quality, product category, and likely serving cost.

Infrastructure cost guidance is there to support pricing judgment. It is not a separate deduction from creator balance.

### Pricing Responsibility

Creators remain responsible for choosing prices that make sense for the product and business model.

## Refunds, Reversals, And Disputes

If a transaction is refunded, reversed, or successfully disputed, the creator's related sale attribution may be adjusted.

Creators should understand that earnings are not insulated from legitimate post-sale corrections.

### Financial Adjustments

Marketplace selling includes the possibility of reversals, refunds, and disputes.

## Enforcement

Edgaze may restrict, suspend, unlist, demonetize, or remove creator products or creator access where necessary.

### Common Reasons

Examples can include:

- policy violations
- fraud or abuse
- rights infringement
- deceptive listings
- payout or compliance issues

### Marketplace Protection

Enforcement exists to protect customers, creators, and the health of the marketplace.
